**Eight athletes from Retford and District Lifesavers are set to compete at the Inter-Club Lifesaving World Championships in South Africa later this year, thanks to support from local councillors.**

The championships take place in Port Elizabeth from 25 November to 13 December 2026, bringing together an estimated 4,000 to 5,000 athletes from around the world. The competition combines lifesaving sport with simulated emergency response challenges across nine categories.

An £881 Councillor Community Grant, contributed to by five Bassetlaw District Councillors, will go towards additional training and championship entry fees for the eight competing members.

Peter Moyes, Head Coach of Retford and District Lifesavers, said: "Thanks to this grant, eight dedicated members of Retford and District Lifesavers will now have the incredible opportunity to compete at the World Lifesavers Championships later this year. Their hard work, commitment and passion for the sport have brought them this far, and this funding ensures they can continue to grow, learn and proudly represent our community on the world stage."

Councillor Carolyn Troop, Ward Member for East Retford South, speaking on behalf of the contributing councillors, added: "We are incredibly proud to support Retford and District Lifesavers as they help these young athletes take a major step towards achieving their dreams. Their passion, discipline and dedication are truly inspiring. We wish them every success as they represent Retford on the world stage."

The grant was contributed to by Cllr Carolyn Troop and Cllr David Naylor (East Retford South), Cllr Dan Henderson (East Retford East), Cllr Harriet Digby (East Retford West) and Cllr Hazel Brand (Misterton).
