Retford may be a relatively small market town, but its central location in the East Midlands means residents are within reach of several major airports. Whether travelling for a family holiday, a business trip or a long haul journey, people living in Retford have multiple airport options within roughly one to two hours of travel.

These airports can usually be reached by car, and some are also accessible by train with a connection from Retford railway station. Below are five of the nearest major airports and how travellers from Retford can get to them.

## Doncaster Sheffield Airport (currently closed)

Doncaster Sheffield Airport was historically the closest commercial airport to Retford, located around 20 miles to the north west. Built on the site of the former RAF Finningley airbase, the airport opened for passenger flights in 2005 and quickly became popular with travellers from Nottinghamshire, South Yorkshire and Lincolnshire.

Before its closure in 2022, the airport offered flights to a range of European destinations including Spain, Portugal, Greece and Cyprus. Many local residents preferred using Doncaster Sheffield because it was smaller and easier to navigate than larger airports.

By car, the airport was typically reachable from Retford in around 30 minutes via the A638 and A614. Public transport options also existed, with train services running from Retford to Doncaster followed by a short bus or taxi journey to the airport.

Although the airport is currently closed, there have been strong efforts from local authorities and government to bring it back into operation. Plans have been announced to reopen the airport within the next few years with new investment and upgraded facilities. If successful, it could once again become the most convenient airport for people living in Retford.

## East Midlands Airport

East Midlands Airport is one of the main airports serving the Midlands and lies roughly 45 miles south west of Retford. The airport offers a wide range of flights across Europe and is particularly popular for holiday travel.

Many budget and charter airlines operate from East Midlands Airport, connecting passengers to destinations such as Spain, Portugal, Turkey and Greece. The airport is also one of the largest cargo hubs in the United Kingdom, with major logistics companies operating freight flights throughout the night.

Driving is the most common way to reach East Midlands Airport from Retford. The journey usually takes around one hour and fifteen minutes via the A1 and M1 motorways.

Travelling by train is also possible. Passengers can take a train from Retford to stations such as Nottingham or Derby and then continue by bus or taxi to the airport. While this route takes longer than driving, it provides a practical option for travellers who prefer not to drive.

## Humberside Airport

Humberside Airport is located about 40 miles to the north east of Retford near the Humber Estuary. Although smaller than some other regional airports, it offers a range of domestic and European flights and plays an important role in supporting offshore energy operations in the North Sea.

For passengers travelling from Retford, the airport can usually be reached by car in around one hour via the A161 and M180. Because of its relatively small size, parking and check in at Humberside Airport are often quicker and less crowded than at larger airports.

Public transport options are more limited than at larger airports, but train travel is still possible. Passengers can take a train from Retford towards Doncaster and continue on to stations in North Lincolnshire, followed by a short taxi journey to the airport.

Humberside Airport is often chosen by travellers who prefer a quieter and more relaxed airport experience.

## Leeds Bradford Airport

Leeds Bradford Airport is located roughly 50 miles north west of Retford and serves the wider Yorkshire region. The airport is considerably larger than Humberside and offers a wide range of flights to destinations across Europe.

Popular routes include holiday destinations in Spain, Italy, the Canary Islands and the Greek islands. Several budget airlines operate from Leeds Bradford, making it a popular choice for affordable European travel.

Driving from Retford to Leeds Bradford Airport typically takes around one hour and thirty minutes depending on traffic, usually via the A1 and M62.

Travelling by train is also an option. Passengers can take a train from Retford to Leeds and then continue to the airport via bus or taxi. While the journey takes longer than driving, it is a straightforward route and works well for travellers heading into the city before catching a flight.

## Manchester Airport

Manchester Airport is the largest airport within reach of Retford and offers the widest range of international destinations. Located around 55 miles to the west, it serves as a major hub for long haul and international travel.

From Manchester Airport, passengers can fly to destinations across Europe as well as North America, the Middle East, Asia and parts of Africa. Because of this extensive network, many travellers from Retford choose Manchester when flying long distance.

Driving to Manchester Airport from Retford usually takes between one hour and thirty minutes and two hours depending on traffic, typically via the A1 and M60.

Train travel is another convenient option. Passengers can take a train from Retford to Manchester, usually with a change at Sheffield, and then connect directly to the airport using the dedicated rail station located beneath the terminal buildings. This makes Manchester one of the easiest major airports to reach by train.

## A Note on Retford Gamston Airport

While Retford does not have its own commercial passenger airport, there is a smaller airfield just outside the town at Gamston.

Retford Gamston Airport is primarily used for private aviation, flying schools and training flights. The airfield has been operating since the mid twentieth century and remains a centre for general aviation activity in the region.

Although it does not offer commercial airline services, it is an important facility for pilot training and private aircraft operations. Local residents sometimes see small aircraft taking off and landing from the runway, which sits just a few miles south of Retford.

## Air Travel Options for Retford Residents

Despite not having a large commercial airport directly in the town, Retford is well placed for air travel thanks to its central location. Within roughly one to two hours by car, travellers can reach several airports offering flights across the UK, Europe and the rest of the world.

The town’s strong road connections and its position on the [East Coast Main Line](/articles/train-travel-retford) also make it relatively easy to reach these airports by train. With the potential reopening of Doncaster Sheffield Airport in the coming years, air travel could become even more convenient for residents of Retford and the surrounding area.
