Whether you've had a long week at work, the fridge is looking decidedly bare, or you simply fancy treating yourself to something special without leaving the sofa, Retford has a surprisingly varied takeaway scene to satisfy just about any craving. From fragrant Chinese dishes and wood-fired pizzas to sizzling kebabs and rich, spiced curries, this Nottinghamshire market town punches well above its weight when it comes to the quality and variety of its takeaway options.

This guide aims to answer the practical questions people usually have first: which places deliver, which ones are collection-only, where you can order online, and which names come up again and again in local conversations. It is best read as a starting point for narrowing the field rather than as a promise that every menu, payment method, or delivery area is unchanged on the day you order.

## How to use this guide

If you already know what cuisine you want, skip straight to that section. If you are still deciding, the quickest way to use this page is to work by ordering style:

- want the easiest app-and-delivery option
- want something more independent than a chain
- need a mixed-order place for a household with different tastes
- are happy to collect if the food is better for it

That matters because takeaway quality in a town like Retford is rarely just about "best overall". It is usually about what kind of order you are placing and how much convenience matters on that particular night.

Chinese Takeaways in Retford
----------------------------

Chinese food remains one of the nation's favourite takeaway choices, and Retford is no exception. There's something deeply comforting about a box of egg fried rice, a portion of crispy chilli beef, or a generous helping of sweet and sour chicken after a long day. Retford has a few solid options in this category, each with their own personality and approach.

### Freds

Freds is something of a local institution, and if you've lived in Retford for any length of time, there's a good chance it's already on your radar. This is a collection-only takeaway, so don't expect a delivery driver turning up at your door --- but the trade-off is that you're getting your food fresh, made to order, and ready to pick up at your convenience.

Payment-wise, Freds accepts both card and cash, which is always appreciated in an era when some smaller takeaways have gone fully cashless (or stubbornly refused to move with the times). They're open every day of the week, which makes them a reliable option regardless of when the craving strikes. To place your order, you'll need to give them a ring --- no app or website ordering here, just good old-fashioned telephone service, which some people actually prefer.

If you've never been and are wondering whether the collection-only model is worth it, the answer is almost certainly yes. There's a reason Freds has built the loyal following it has.

### Lucky Days

Lucky Days takes a more traditional approach in one important respect: it's cash only. If you're the sort of person who never carries notes anymore, make sure you've been to the cashpoint before you order. The upside is that Lucky Days does deliver, which means you can enjoy their menu from the comfort of your own home without needing to factor in a trip out.

Orders are placed by phone, keeping things simple and personal. It's worth noting that cash-only takeaways have become less common in recent years, so if you're a regular customer, keeping a bit of cash in the kitchen drawer purely for Lucky Days purposes isn't a bad idea.

### Thai Bowl

Thai Bowl is slightly different from the other two, offering a more contemporary ordering experience alongside its food. You can order both online and by phone, payment is accepted by card or cash, and delivery is available --- making it the most flexible of the three options in terms of how you engage with it.

Thai Bowl brings a broader Southeast Asian influence to the table, which provides a welcome bit of variety for those nights when you want something a little different from the standard takeaway repertoire.

Pizza Takeaways in Retford
--------------------------

Pizza is arguably the reigning champion of the takeaway world, and Retford caters well to fans of a good slice. Whether you're after a no-fuss franchise pizza, an authentically fired artisan pie, or something somewhere in between, there's an option here for you.

### Domino's

No UK takeaway guide would be complete without acknowledging Domino's. The global franchise needs little introduction --- you know what you're getting, and that's largely the point. The app is slick, the deals are frequent (keep an eye out for the various online vouchers and combo offers that are almost always running), and delivery is reliable.

Domino's is the go-to when you want familiarity, speed, and a wide range of customisable options. It's particularly well-suited to feeding a group --- whether that's a family film night, a gathering of friends, or just a household where everyone has a slightly different topping preference.

### Saucy Pizza Co

Now, if you're after something that takes the concept of pizza a bit more seriously, Saucy Pizza Co is well worth your attention. This is authentic, pizza-oven-fired pizza --- the kind with a proper char on the base, bubbling cheese, and toppings that taste like they've been sourced with care rather than bulk-ordered from a catering supplier.

Saucy Pizza Co offers both delivery and collection, and you can order online, making the whole process straightforward. This is the sort of pizza that makes you wonder why you ever settled for anything less. If you're a pizza enthusiast who believes that the method of cooking matters --- and it absolutely does --- then Saucy Pizza Co deserves a prominent place in your Retford takeaway rotation.

### Valentinos

Valentinos occupies an interesting middle ground. Technically a pizzeria, it also ventures into kebab and burger territory, which makes it a versatile choice for households where not everyone is in the mood for pizza. You can order online, and both delivery and collection are available.

The range of options at Valentinos makes it a particularly handy option for those mixed-preference situations --- one person wants a margarita, another fancies a kebab, and somebody else is asking about burgers. Rather than ordering from multiple places, Valentinos can often cover all bases in a single order.

Indian Takeaways in Retford
---------------------------

Indian cuisine is consistently one of the most popular takeaway categories in Britain, and with good reason. A well-made curry, a fluffy naan, a portion of perfectly spiced rice --- it's the sort of meal that feels simultaneously comforting and exciting. Retford has a strong selection of Indian takeaways, all of which offer online ordering and delivery alongside collection.

### Everest Tandoori

Everest Tandoori is a well-established name in Retford's Indian takeaway scene. Tandoori cooking --- named after the clay oven used to cook the dishes --- produces wonderfully smoky, charred flavours that you simply can't replicate on a standard hob, and Everest delivers on this front. They offer online ordering, delivery, and collection, making the whole experience as convenient as possible.

Whether you're after a mild, creamy korma, a fiery vindaloo, or something in between like a classic tikka masala or a fragrant jalfrezi, Everest Tandoori has the menu depth to satisfy most preferences.

### Maira's Kitchen

Maira's Kitchen brings a slightly different flavour profile to Retford's Indian takeaway offering. Again, everything you'd hope for in terms of convenience is present --- online ordering, delivery, and collection --- but the emphasis here feels a little more home-style, with dishes that have a warmth and care to them that distinguishes them from the more standard curry-house output.

For those who appreciate a more personal touch in their Indian cooking, Maira's Kitchen is well worth exploring. It's the sort of place that tends to build a loyal following amongst those who stumble across it and then wonder how they ever managed without it.

### Gurkha Tandoori

Gurkha Tandoori adds a Nepalese dimension to Retford's Indian takeaway scene, which is a genuinely exciting prospect for food lovers. While the menus often overlap with traditional Indian takeaway staples, Nepalese-influenced establishments frequently bring dishes and flavour combinations that you won't find elsewhere --- think slightly different spice profiles, different preparations, and occasionally entirely unique dishes.

Like the others in this category, Gurkha Tandoori offers online ordering, delivery, and collection. If you haven't explored the Gurkha menu yet, it's well worth branching out from your usual order.

Kebabs, Burgers & Grills in Retford
-----------------------------------

Late nights, post-pub hunger, or just the undeniable appeal of something grilled, sauced, and wrapped --- Retford's kebab and burger options are solid, and there are a couple of names you'll want to know.

### Topkapi

Topkapi is a one-stop shop for the late-night (or any-night) craving for something hearty and satisfying. Their menu covers burgers, kebabs, and pizza, so there's genuine variety on offer. Both delivery and collection are available, and you can order online, which means you can have your doner or dirty burger en route to your door without even picking up the phone.

In a category that's sometimes unfairly dismissed as being purely for the post-pub crowd, Topkapi demonstrates that a good kebab is a good kebab regardless of the time of day or level of sobriety.

### Charcoal Grill

Charcoal Grill covers much the same territory as Topkapi --- burgers, kebabs, and pizza, with delivery and collection both available, and online ordering to make it easy. The name gives you a clue about the approach: grilled, charred, smoky food with that particular quality you get when meat meets open heat properly.

Having two solid options in this category is genuinely useful, because on busy weekend nights when demand is high, having an alternative means you're less likely to be staring at a long estimated delivery time.

Special Mention: MeatCastles --- Retford's Viral Burger Van
---------------------------------------------------------

![MeatCastles](/images/meatcastles.webp)

Now, this one is a little different from the rest of the guide, but it absolutely deserves its place here. MeatCastles is a burger van operation that has built a genuine following thanks to the kind of burgers that end up all over social media --- big, bold, stacked creations that photograph beautifully and, crucially, taste even better than they look.

The van tends to operate around Retford at weekends, so if you're out and about on a Saturday or Sunday and spot the MeatCastles van, do not walk past it. Beyond that, MeatCastles makes regular appearances at the Clumber Inn pub in nearby Ordsall, typically a couple of times a month. It's worth following them on social media to keep up with exactly where they'll be and when, as these appearances are often announced in advance and tend to draw a crowd.

The viral burger concept is one that's swept across the UK in recent years, with independent burger traders producing food that puts many established restaurants to shame. MeatCastles fits squarely into this category --- this is not your average van burger. These are proper, considered creations, and if you haven't tried them yet, putting them on your Retford food bucket list is highly recommended.

A Few Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Retford Takeaway Experience
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

### Plan Around Peak Times

Saturday evenings are, unsurprisingly, the busiest time for takeaway orders across the board. If you're planning a Saturday night in, it's worth ordering a little earlier than you think you need to --- delivery times can stretch significantly during peak hours, and there's little worse than a grumbling stomach while you watch your estimated arrival time creep further and further into the evening.

Midweek orders, particularly on Tuesday and Wednesday evenings, tend to come through much more quickly and with fresher-feeling service. If flexibility is an option, it's worth bearing in mind.

### Keep Cash Available for Cash-Only Places

As noted in the Lucky Days entry above, not every takeaway in Retford has fully embraced card payments. Keeping a small amount of cash available means you're never caught out when the mood for a Lucky Days order strikes without warning.

### Use Online Ordering Where Available

For the takeaways that offer online ordering --- Thai Bowl, Saucy Pizza Co, Valentinos, Everest Tandoori, Maira's Kitchen, Gurkha Tandoori, Topkapi, and Charcoal Grill --- placing your order via their websites or apps has real advantages. You can browse the full menu at your own pace, spot deals or meal offers you might have missed over the phone, and have a written record of exactly what you've ordered if anything goes wrong.

It also removes the sometimes-awkward experience of trying to pronounce a dish name you've never said out loud before whilst someone waits patiently on the other end of the line.

### Don't Overlook Collection

Collection is often faster than delivery, particularly during busy periods. If you're able to hop in the car and pick up your order, you'll frequently have it in your hands --- and on your table --- significantly faster than waiting for a driver. Places like Freds are collection-only for a reason: it keeps the food quality consistent and the operation streamlined.

### Follow MeatCastles on Social Media

Seriously --- if you're a burger fan, this one is genuinely important. MeatCastles' appearances are announced through their social channels, and given that they operate at specific locations on specific dates rather than from a fixed premises, staying in the loop is the only reliable way to make sure you don't miss them.
